\begin{cabstract}
  随着新能源汽车向智能化发展，车载系统的服务边界正由车内延伸至车外真实场景。针对传统雨伞需手持、占用双手的问题，本文设计并实现了一种以充气结构为支撑的车载多形态飞行机器人系统——“飞行雨伞”。该系统以车辆为地面部署基准，可在收纳态与飞行遮蔽态间实施主动形态调整，为用户下车遇雨时提供“随车常备、快速部署、双手解放”的遮雨体验。

  本文首先完成了“母机-子机”分布式协作的系统总体架构设计。在硬件层面，创新性地采用可折叠薄壁充气气柱作为无人机机臂与雨伞骨架，通过气压充驱动实现形变，并对薄壁压力容器进行了力学建模与仿真校核；动力系统通过动量理论完成了大惯量整定。在软件层面，基于PX4构建了飞控系统，并引入降增益PID控制策略以应对超大转动惯量带来的控制挑战，实现了稳定悬停。实验结果表明，原型机结构轻量、部署迅速，有效验证了车载多形态飞行Agent在离车服务场景下的可行性与工程应用价值。
\end{cabstract}

\ckeywords{多旋翼飞行器,充气结构,形态重构,车载服务系统,PID控制}

\begin{eabstract}
  With the intelligent evolution of new energy vehicles, the service boundary of vehicle-borne systems is expanding from the cockpit to real-world outdoor scenarios. Addressing the limitation of traditional umbrellas that require manual holding and occupy both hands, this thesis designs and implements a vehicle-borne morphing flying robot system supported by an inflatable structure, named "Flying Umbrella". Utilizing the vehicle as a ground deployed baseline, the system executes active morphing adjustments between a stowed state and a flying shielding state, aiming to provide users with a "vehicle-on-board, rapidly deployed, and hands-free" rain-sheltering experience during inclement weather commutes.

  Firstly, a distributed cooperative system architecture based on a "host-client" mechanism is developed. At the hardware level, thin-walled foldable inflatable air-columns are innovatively utilized as both the drone arms and the umbrella frame, with deformation driven by pneumatic pressure. Mechanical modeling and simulation verification are conducted based on the thin-walled pressure vessel theory, and the propulsion system is tuned via momentum theory to accommodate large inertia. At the software level, the flight control system is constructed upon PX4, introducing a reduced-gain PID control strategy to address the severe control challenges induced by the ultra-large rotational inertia, thereby achieving stable hovering. Experimental results demonstrate that the prototype features a lightweight structure and rapid deployment, effectively validating the feasibility and engineering application value of vehicle-borne morphing flying agents in off-board service scenarios.
\end{eabstract}
